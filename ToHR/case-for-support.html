02Why This Matters

Black Rock City is remote by design. Care has to travel in with us.

Offline by nature

Cell coverage out there is unreliable, and that's part of the place. So the guide simply lives on the device, whole and ready, no signal needed.

Knowledge is rare until it's urgent

Most people have never practiced these moments. Clear, calm steps in hand mean nobody has to already be an expert to take good care of someone.

Hesitation is human

People in expanded states sometimes wait before reaching out. A private, nonjudgmental guide lowers that threshold and meets them where they are.

The city moves

Camps relocate and services change year to year. Verified, dated information honors people's time and trust.

03The Solution

A field guide that works when nothing else does.

Install once at the gate. After that it needs no signal, no account, and no trust in anyone's server. It helps people make better decisions faster, escalate sooner, and find verified human help.

Offline-first

A progressive web app that fully works in airplane mode: protocols, map, directories, everything cached on the device.

Emergency-first

Help Now is one tap from every screen. Short cards and decision trees, built for panic, dust, glare, and 4% battery.

Verified-only trust

Nothing is shown as confirmed unless verified for 2026. Our logo appears only beside verified Temple locations, so the mark itself becomes wayfinding.

04The Product

Six sections, one hive.

The interface is a honeycomb: each cell is one kind of knowledge, and exactly one cell at a time is promoted as the suggested next step. Help Now protocols, an offline map with clock-street lookup, the verified Temple node directory, drug testing guides, know-your-rights field scripts, and consent and boundaries guidance.

Four display modes ship on day one: Dark for evenings, Bright Sun for noon, High Contrast Emergency for crisis use, and Night, red-shifted to preserve night vision. You're looking at them right now, via the hex selector above.

06The Human Network

Software finds the help. Humans are the help.

Temple of Harm Reduction operates verified nodes across the city: calm spaces, peer support, supplies, and testing support. The app is the connective tissue: every node is registered before the event, verified for 2026, dated, and tagged by service. Node operators follow clear escalation expectations, routing people to Rangers, ESD, and medical whenever danger is possible.

Registered

Nodes sign up before the event with location, services, and hours.

Verified

A human confirms each node for the current year. The Held Drop badge means checked.

Accountable

Clear boundaries and escalation rules govern every node operator.

08Beyond 2026

One event pack today. A platform after.

The architecture separates the app from its content: Burning Man 2026 is simply the first content pack. The same shell then serves festivals, cities, and sister church work, each as a downloadable pack with its own verified locations and protocols.

Multi-event packs

Any gathering can carry a verified pack: same trust system, new map.

Languages

Spanish first, then additional languages, plus audio read-aloud mode.

Print & reach

Printable emergency cards and install flyers extend the guide to dead phones and no phones.
