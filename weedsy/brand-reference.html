01 · Identity

The Seal

A round produce sticker holding a rising sun over a furrow. No leaf, no cross, no smoke. Presented on white, the way it would sit on a jar at the farmers market.

The sticker

The little seal on an heirloom tomato. Picked by hand, checked by a person, sold by the grower. The perforated inner ring is the stamp of that check.

The sun

Sungrown, not warehouse grown. The half disc rising is harvest morning, and it gives the brand its active color, sungrown amber.

The furrow

One line of living soil. The quiet nod to regenerative growing and to the people who keep the ground healthy.

The seal appears only beside verified farms. It never decorates. Wherever you see it, a human has checked the license and met the grower.

05 · How Weedsy Behaves

Market rules, written down

The brand commitments, drawn straight from the product rules.

Gate first, always

Nothing renders before the 21+ gate. The gate may suggest a starting theme from the visitor's answers, and that suggestion is a UI hint, not a record. What is collected or kept is decided by counsel, not by code.

Green means verified

The seal and the green badge appear only where a human has checked the license and met the grower. Everything unverified says so, honestly and in plain words.

Plain words, no claims

Farmers market voice. No medical claims, no wellness promises, no slang. Any copy that wanders toward a claim ships with a counsel review flag, not a guess.

One brand, many comforts

Classic, Fresh, and Care change accent, spacing, and type size. They never change layout, navigation, or content. Every screen works in all three themes, in large text, and with reduced motion.
